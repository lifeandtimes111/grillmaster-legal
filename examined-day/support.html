Frequently asked

What is the daily practice?
Two moments a day. In the morning you read a passage, set an intention, and sort what's in your control. At night you look back with Seneca's three questions: what did I do badly, what did I do well, what could I do better?

What's free and what's paid?
The daily passage and the full morning-and-evening practice are free forever. The one-time unlock adds the complete source library (Marcus Aurelius, Epictetus, Seneca), the practice tools, themed tracks, and journal history & export.

Is it really a one-time purchase?
Yes. One purchase, yours forever, on every device signed in to your Apple Account. No subscription.

How do I restore my unlock on a new device?
Open the Library tab, tap to unlock, then choose "Restore Purchase."

Where are my reflections stored?
Only on your device. Nothing you write is sent to us or to any server.

Why do the readings look a little old-fashioned?
Every passage comes from a public-domain translation of the original Stoic texts, quoted faithfully.
